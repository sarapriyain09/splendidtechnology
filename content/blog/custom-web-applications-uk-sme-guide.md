# Custom Web Applications for UK SMEs: A Practical Guide

If you run a small or medium business in the UK and you're relying on a combination of spreadsheets, email threads, and off-the-shelf tools that don't quite fit — a custom web application might be exactly what you need.

This guide covers what custom web apps actually are, when they make sense, what they cost, and how to find the right web developer in the UK.

---

## What Is a Custom Web Application?

A custom web application is software built specifically for your business — not a generic platform like Shopify or Xero, but something designed around your exact workflows, users, and data.

Examples include:

- **Customer portals** — where clients can log in, view orders, raise tickets, or access documents.
- **Internal tools** — job scheduling, stock management, reporting dashboards.
- **SaaS platforms** — software you sell to your own customers as a subscription service.
- **Field engineer apps** — mobile-first tools for technicians to log work, capture readings, and submit reports.
- **Booking and quoting systems** — replacing back-and-forth emails with a structured, automated flow.

The key difference from off-the-shelf software: a custom web app does exactly what you need, integrates with your existing systems, and you own the codebase.

---

## When Does a Custom Web Application Make Sense?

You probably don't need a custom web app if an existing tool does the job well. But you likely do need one when:

- You're managing a process entirely in spreadsheets and it's causing errors or delays.
- You've tried 3–4 SaaS tools and none of them quite fit your workflow.
- Your customers need a portal or self-service tool that no generic platform provides.
- You want to build and sell your own software product.
- You have data in multiple disconnected systems and need them to talk to each other.

For UK SMEs specifically, custom web apps often replace a combination of manual admin work, email chains, and generic tools — reducing overhead and improving the customer experience at the same time.

## SaaS Development UK: Building Your Own Product

SaaS (Software as a Service) development means building a web application that you charge customers to use — typically on a monthly or annual subscription.

If you have domain expertise and a process that other businesses in your sector struggle with, a SaaS product can be a significant revenue stream.

The technical stack matters. Modern SaaS platforms built on Next.js, React, Node.js, and PostgreSQL are faster to build, easier to scale, and cheaper to run than older frameworks. A good UK SaaS development team will help you think through:

- **Multi-tenancy** — each customer sees only their own data.
- **Subscription billing** — integrated with Stripe or similar.
- **User roles and permissions** — admins, managers, read-only users.
- **Analytics and reporting** — usage data, dashboards, exports.
- **API integrations** — connecting your SaaS to the tools your customers already use.
