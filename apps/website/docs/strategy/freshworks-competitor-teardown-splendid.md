# Freshworks competitor teardown for Splendid Technology (90-day focus)

Date: 2026-06-17
Primary competitor reviewed: Freshworks homepage and top-level product messaging

## Executive summary

Freshworks wins by combining simple product packaging, enterprise-grade trust signals, and outcome-led AI messaging.

For the next 90 days, Splendid should not copy enterprise breadth. Splendid should beat Freshworks on SME relevance, speed of execution, and hands-on implementation support.

## 1) Freshworks strengths to emulate

### 1. Outcome-first messaging

Freshworks leads with business outcomes, not feature dumps.

What to emulate:
- Write headlines around outcomes: lead conversion, response speed, missed follow-up recovery, team productivity.
- Use concrete timeline language: "in 30-90 days" and "within 14 days".

### 2. Product packaging by job to be done

Freshworks structures offers by team/use case (IT, business teams, operations) rather than technical architecture.

What to emulate:
- Keep two clear pillars only: CRM Solutions and AI & Business Automation.
- Under each pillar, name practical use cases before tools.

### 3. Strong conversion architecture

Freshworks uses dual CTAs throughout: free trial and demo.

What to emulate:
- Primary CTA: Book Demo.
- Secondary CTA: See Use Cases (or View Case Studies).
- Repeat CTA pair in hero, middle, and end sections.

### 4. Heavy trust proof

Freshworks repeatedly shows customer logos, customer stories, and quantified outcomes.

What to emulate:
- Add 3-5 measurable KPI snippets on homepage.
- Include short case stories with role/company context.
- Build a visible case-study section in nav and homepage.

### 5. Embedded AI narrative

AI is presented as part of workflows, not as a standalone novelty.

What to emulate:
- Position AI as integrated with CRM and operations workflows.
- Show where AI helps, where rules-based automation is better, and how governance works.

## 2) Gaps Splendid can exploit (SME wedge)

### Gap A: Enterprise-first language can feel distant to SMEs

Freshworks language and examples are often enterprise-oriented.

Splendid advantage:
- Speak directly to SMEs, engineering firms, service businesses, and consultants.
- Use practical terms: enquiry-to-quote delays, missed callbacks, spreadsheet handoffs, follow-up leakage.

### Gap B: Implementation confidence gap for smaller teams

SMEs often need guided setup, not just software access.

Splendid advantage:
- Emphasize hands-on implementation and workflow design.
- Offer clear rollout promises (for example, 2-week pilot and 6-week implementation).

### Gap C: Generic trust proof vs local relevance

Global logos are powerful but can feel less relatable for regional SME buyers.

Splendid advantage:
- Use local and sector-relevant case studies.
- Show before/after metrics from SME sales and operations contexts.

### Gap D: Product breadth can create decision fatigue

Large product suites can overwhelm buyers who just want a practical first step.

Splendid advantage:
- Keep website and offers tightly focused on 2 pillars.
- Present a simple "start here" pathway:
  1) CRM baseline
  2) Workflow automation
  3) AI assistants and advanced integrations

## 6) 30-day execution checklist

Week 1:
- Update homepage hero and pillar copy
- Ship simplified navigation
- Add dual CTA pattern (Book Demo, See Use Cases)

Week 2:
- Publish one CRM case story and one AI automation case story
- Add KPI proof strip with available real metrics

Week 3:
- Launch dedicated pillar landing pages: CRM and AI Automation

Week 4:
- Instrument conversion events (demo requests, contact form completion, CTA clicks)
- Review message-to-lead quality fit and iterate copy
