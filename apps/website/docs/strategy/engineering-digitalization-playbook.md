# Splendid Technology - Engineering Digitalization Playbook

Date: 2026-06-08

This playbook reframes Splendid from an engineering-services-led company to a technology company for engineering and manufacturing SMEs.

## 1) Homepage Hero + Full Messaging Rewrite

### Brand Positioning

Splendid Technology helps engineering and manufacturing SMEs digitize sales, operations, assets, and engineering workflows.

Engineering expertise remains core, but software-enabled business outcomes become the primary story.

### Homepage Metadata (recommended)

- Title: Digital Solutions for Engineering and Manufacturing SMEs | Splendid Technology
- Meta description: Splendid Technology helps UK engineering and manufacturing SMEs digitize CRM, operations, ERP workflows, IoT assets, and engineering intelligence systems.
- H1 keyword cluster: engineering digitalization UK, manufacturing digital transformation UK, CRM ERP IoT engineering workflows

### Homepage Hero (copy-ready)

- Eyebrow: Engineering Digitalization for UK SMEs
- H1: Digital Solutions for Engineering and Manufacturing Businesses
- Subheadline: CRM, ERP, IoT, AI, and Engineering Intelligence to improve decisions, reduce downtime, and scale operations.
- Primary CTA: Book a Digitalization Assessment
- Secondary CTA: Explore Solutions
- Trust line: Built by engineers. Delivered as practical software systems.

### Homepage Section Flow (full messaging architecture)

#### Section A - Problem Framing

Headline: Most engineering SMEs run critical operations across disconnected tools.

Body: Sales data, production updates, maintenance records, and engineering knowledge often live in separate systems. This creates delays, poor visibility, and avoidable cost.

#### Section B - Outcome Promise

Headline: One connected digital layer across sales, operations, and assets.

Body: We design and implement practical systems that connect CRM, operations, IoT telemetry, and engineering workflows into one decision-ready environment.

Outcome bullets:
- Faster quoting and follow-up
- Better production and inventory visibility
- Reduced unplanned downtime
- More reliable engineering decisions

#### Section C - Solution Pillars (cards)

Card 1: Sales and Customer Systems
- CRM implementation
- Lead capture and follow-up workflows
- Customer and quote management
- Commercial dashboarding

Card 2: Operations and ERP Workflows
- Job and work-order tracking
- Inventory and warehouse visibility
- ERP module implementation
- Workflow automation and approvals

Card 3: Industrial IoT and Asset Intelligence
- Condition monitoring and edge devices
- Asset performance tracking
- Predictive maintenance workflows
- Alerting and maintenance dashboards

Card 4: Engineering Intelligence
- Engineering data management
- Design and document automation
- Engineering analytics and reporting
- AI-assisted engineering workflows

#### Section D - Differentiation Strip

Headline: Software companies rarely understand engineering operations. Engineering firms rarely build scalable software.

Body: Splendid combines both, giving engineering and manufacturing SMEs a practical digitalization partner.

#### Section E - Productized Offers Preview

Headline: Start with a focused package, then scale.

Offer chips:
- CRM Accelerator
- Operations Control Starter
- Asset Insight Pilot
- Engineering Intelligence Sprint

#### Section F - Proof / Case Outcomes

Headline: Business outcomes, not technical vanity metrics.

KPI examples:
- Reduced follow-up lag by 40 percent
- Improved maintenance planning accuracy by 25 percent
- Cut manual reporting effort by 60 percent
- Increased quote-to-order conversion by 15 percent

#### Section G - Final CTA

Headline: Get a digitalization roadmap in 14 days.

Body: We assess your current systems, identify priority bottlenecks, and propose a phased implementation plan with ROI checkpoints.

Primary CTA: Book a Digitalization Assessment
Secondary CTA: Talk to an Engineer

### Messaging Guardrails

- Lead with outcomes, not disciplines.
- Use engineering as credibility and implementation depth.
- Avoid positioning language like generic engineering services.
- Prefer language like digital systems, operational intelligence, connected workflows, and measurable business outcomes.

## 3) 90-Day GTM Plan (Offers, Pricing Anchors, Funnel)

### Strategic Objective

Generate qualified pipeline for digitalization-led engagements while preserving engineering-led credibility.

### Offer Ladder (productized)

1. Digitalization Assessment (entry)
- Timeline: 2 weeks
- Deliverable: current-state map, bottleneck analysis, 90-day roadmap
- Pricing anchor: GBP 1,500 to GBP 3,000

2. System Starter Implementation (core)
- Timeline: 4 to 8 weeks
- Deliverable: one connected workflow (example: CRM + quoting, or IoT + maintenance dashboard)
- Pricing anchor: GBP 8,000 to GBP 20,000

3. Operations Intelligence Program (expansion)
- Timeline: 8 to 16 weeks
- Deliverable: cross-functional rollout across sales, operations, and asset data
- Pricing anchor: GBP 25,000 to GBP 75,000

4. Managed Optimization Retainer (recurring)
- Monthly service covering improvements, dashboards, and process tuning
- Pricing anchor: GBP 1,500 to GBP 6,000 per month

- Update homepage and services messaging to digitalization-first language.

  - Digitalization Assessment
  - CRM and Sales Systems Starter

  - 20 targeted outreach messages/week

  - topic: How engineering SMEs can connect sales, operations, and maintenance data

Targets:
- 20 qualified calls cumulative
- 6 paid assessments cumulative
- 2 starter implementation projects

- Convert successful starter projects into named case studies.
- Introduce standardized delivery playbooks by offer type.
- Launch referral motion with existing clients and partners.

Targets:
