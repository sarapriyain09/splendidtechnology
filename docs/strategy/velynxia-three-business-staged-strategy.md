# Velynxia Three-Business Staged Strategy

Status: Proposed
Date: 2026-07-04
Owner: Velynxia Leadership

## Executive Summary
Velynxia should operate as three coordinated businesses, each funding the next level of capability.

1. Business 1: SaaS Revenue Engine (now)
2. Business 2: Engineering Software Platform (2-4 years)
3. Business 3: Engineering Hardware Platform (4-10 years)

Capital Flow Principle:
- Business 1 funds Business 2.
- Business 2 funds Business 3.

## Portfolio Definition

### Business 1: SaaS Revenue Engine
Purpose:
- Generate recurring cash flow and predictable operating capacity.

Core products:
- Growth Platform
- AI Media Suite
- Commerce Platform (product-centric operating system)

Operating outcomes:
- Monthly recurring revenue growth
- Reliable go-to-market execution loops
- Productized AI workflows with approval and governance

### Business 2: Engineering Software Platform
Purpose:
- Build defensible, high-value engineering software where domain expertise is a moat.

Studio model:
- Motor Studio
- Generator Studio
- Drive Train Studio
- Motion Studio
- Digital Twin Studio
- Reliability Studio
- Asset Health Studio
- Engineering AI

Supporting platform capabilities:
- Simulation services (thermal, mechanical, electrical)
- Engineering knowledge graph and retrieval
- Model lifecycle and experiment tracking
- Domain copilots and workflow orchestration

### Business 3: Engineering Hardware Platform
Purpose:
- Build reusable hardware and controls infrastructure that can power multiple end applications.

Flagship direction:
- Velynxia Motion Controller platform

Platform modules:
- Motion Controller
- Motor Drive
- Sensor Hub
- Condition Monitoring
- Industrial IoT
- Robotics Controller
- Vision Controller
- Edge AI Controller
- Development Kits

Go-to-market wedge:
- Motion Lab Kit for universities, research institutes, robotics startups, and industrial engineering teams.

## Strategic Positioning
Positioning statement:
- Velynxia builds the platform that products, machines, and robots run on.

Not a single-device strategy:
- Avoid product identity lock-in around one end device.
- Prioritize reusable controller, software, telemetry, and diagnostics building blocks.

## Time-Phased Strategy

### Phase A: 2026-2027
Focus:
- Complete Growth Platform and AI Media Suite to strengthen recurring revenue.
- Mature Commerce Platform as product-first operating layer.

Exit criteria:
- Stable monthly recurring revenue trend
- Production-grade platform reliability and observability
- Repeatable customer onboarding and retention motion

### Phase B: 2027-2028
Focus:
- Launch Engineering Platform foundation.
- Deliver Engineering AI, Motor Studio, and Digital Twin initial releases.

Exit criteria:
- First paying design partners for engineering software
- Demonstrable workflow time savings for engineering tasks
- Reusable data and model contracts across studios

### Phase C: 2028-2030
Focus:
- Release Motion Controller Kit and diagnostics modules.
- Expand sensor and condition monitoring capabilities.

Exit criteria:
- Successful pilot deployments in education or R&D
- Stable SDK + dashboard developer experience
- Hardware reliability and support playbook validated

### Phase D: 2030+
Focus:
- Expand into robotics and advanced motion verticals driven by customer demand.
- Specialize by high-value industry use cases.

Exit criteria:
- Portfolio-level profitability across software + hardware mix
- Repeatable deployment architecture and partner ecosystem

## Platform Architecture Guardrails
This strategy must follow Velynxia platform rules:

- Keep modules plugin-based and event-driven.
- Keep provider abstractions for AI, RAG, memory, and tool layers.
- Keep cross-app capabilities in shared platform services.
- Avoid hardcoded vendor coupling in business modules.
- Use approval workflows for high-impact automated actions.

## Operating Model and Investment Logic

### Investment sequencing
- Allocate majority of near-term delivery effort to Business 1 cash engines.
- Allocate staged R&D bandwidth to Business 2 capabilities with partner validation.
- Gate Business 3 spend by software traction, not by narrative ambition.

### Team shape evolution
- Stage 1: SaaS product and growth execution teams
- Stage 2: Engineering software domain and simulation teams
- Stage 3: Controls, embedded, hardware validation, and field support teams

## KPI Framework

### Business 1 KPIs
- Monthly recurring revenue
- Net revenue retention
- Gross margin
- Customer activation time

### Business 2 KPIs
- Engineering workflow time reduction
- Design iteration cycle-time improvement
- Accuracy and reliability of simulation and twin outputs
- Paid design partner conversion

### Business 3 KPIs
- Controller reliability and uptime
- Kit adoption and repeat orders
- SDK adoption and developer retention
- Field issue mean-time-to-resolution

## Immediate 90-Day Actions
1. Lock portfolio narrative in product and investor-facing messaging.
2. Prioritize Business 1 roadmap execution and reliability work.
3. Define Engineering Platform architecture and initial contracts for Motor Studio and Digital Twin.
4. Start design-partner discovery interviews for engineering software use cases.
5. Draft Motion Controller Kit concept spec, bill of materials assumptions, and pilot criteria.

## Decision
Adopt a staged three-business strategy where current SaaS execution is the commercial foundation for engineering software, which then funds a reusable engineering hardware platform.
