## Issue 4 - Phase 4: 2030+ Domain Expansion

Title:
Phase 4 - Robotics and Advanced Motion Expansion (Platform-Led)

Labels:
strategy, portfolio, expansion, robotics, advanced-motion, phase-4, priority-medium

Assignees (suggested):
- Owner: Portfolio Lead
- Support: Engineering Platform Lead
- Support: Hardware Platform Lead
- Support: Partner Ecosystem Lead

Body:

### Objective
Expand into robotics and advanced motion domains using the same controller and studio foundations, driven by customer demand and validated economics.

### Scope
- Identify and prioritize vertical opportunities.
- Build application modules on top of existing controller and software studios.
- Develop integration and deployment partner ecosystem.

### Product and ecosystem checklist
- [ ] Define vertical selection framework (market size, fit, margins, complexity).
- [ ] Prioritize first expansion verticals based on customer evidence.
- [ ] Build modular adapters for selected domain workflows.
- [ ] Create partner certification and integration standards.
- [ ] Package reusable deployment blueprints for field operations.
- [ ] Establish portfolio-level profitability dashboard across software and hardware lines.
- [ ] Run annual architecture review to protect platform reuse and avoid siloed forks.

### Acceptance criteria
- [ ] At least one expansion vertical launched on shared platform primitives.
- [ ] Partner-led deployments active with measurable customer outcomes.
- [ ] Portfolio mix demonstrates sustainable profitability trend.
- [ ] Platform reuse remains high across new modules.

### Non-functional requirements
- [ ] Governance process ensures no cross-tenant or cross-customer data leakage.
- [ ] Shared observability across software-hardware-operational stack.
- [ ] Compliance and safety checkpoints defined for domain-specific rollouts.

### Dependencies
- Stable Business 1 and Business 2 operating performance.
- Hardware and software release management maturity.

### Risks
- Expansion into too many verticals can dilute execution.
- Custom one-off solutions may erode platform leverage.

### Definition of done
- [ ] Expansion motion repeatable with partner ecosystem support.
- [ ] Annual portfolio review confirms strategy remains economically and technically scalable.
